 The first man in space (1 orbit).

 First American in space (suborbital flight).
Mercury-Redstone-4 (USA) Virgil Grissom

21.07.1961

15 min 37 s

 Second American suborbital flight. After splashdown, the ship sank, Grissom managed to get out of it and waited for rescuers in the water.
Vostok-2 (USSR) German Titov

6.08.1961

1 day 1 hour 18 minutes

 The first flight lasting more than a day (17 orbits).
Mercury-Atlas-6 (USA) John Glenn

20.02.1962

4 h 55 min

 First American orbital flight (3 orbits).
Mercury-Atlas-7 (USA) Malcolm Carpenter

24.05.1962

4 h 56 min

 Manual braking control for deorbiting. Splashdown 400 kilometers from the calculated point.
Vostok-3 (USSR) Andriyan Nikolaev

11.08.1962

3 days 22 hours 22 minutes

 First group space flight (together with Vostok-4).
Vostok-4 (USSR) Pavel Popovych

12.08.1962

2 days 22 hours 57 minutes

 The first group space flight. On the first orbit, the ship passed within 5 km of Vostok-3.
Mercury-Atlas-8 (USA) Walter  Schirra

3.10.1962

9 h 13 min

 Development of methods for long-term space flights (6 orbits).
Mercury-Atlas-9 (USA) Gordon Cooper

15.05.1963

1 day 10 hours 20 minutes

 The first American manned flight lasting more than a day (22 orbits). Due to the failure of a number of spacecraft systems, a manual deorbit was performed.
Vostok-5 (USSR) Valery Bykovsky

14.06.1963

4 days 23 hours 6 minutes

 Second group space flight (together with Vostok-6).
Vostok-6 (USSR) Valentina Tereshkova

16.06.1963

2 days 22 hours 50 minutes

 The first woman in space. The ship approached Vostok-5 up to 5 km.
Voskhod-1 (USSR) Vladimir Komarov, Konstantin Feoktistov, Boris Egorov

12.10.1964

1 day 17 min

 Modified Vostok-class ship. The first multi-seat spacecraft. First flight without spacesuits.
Voskhod-2 (USSR) Pavel Belyaev, Alexey Leonov

8.03.1965

1 day 2 hours 2 minutes

 First spacewalk (Leonov) lasting 10 minutes.
Gemini-3 (USA) Virgil Grissom, John Young

23.03.1965

4 h 53 min

 The first American two-seat spacecraft. The first manned spacecraft with the capability of orbital maneuvers. The first on-board computer that made it possible to calculate maneuvers.
Gemini-4 (USA) James McDivitt, Edward White

3.06.1965

4 days 1 hour 56 min

 The first American spacewalk (White, 21 minutes). The first American four-day flight. Manual braking control for deorbiting due to computer failure.
Gemini-5 (USA) Gordon Cooper, Charles Conrad

21.08.1965

7 days 22 hours 56 minutes

 First use of fuel cells to power ship systems. Testing control and navigation systems.
Gemini-7 (USA) Frank Borman, James Lovell

4.12.1965

13 days 18 hours 35 minutes

 Longest American manned flight for the next eight years (206 orbits). Record duration of flight to Soyuz-9. Rendezvous with Gemini 6.
Gemini-6 (USA) Walter  Schirra, Thomas Stafford

15.12.1965

1 day 1 hour 51 min

 The first approach of two manned spacecraft (at a distance of up to 2 meters).
Gemini-8 (USA) Neil Armstrong, David Scott

16.03.1966

10 h 41 min

 The first docking of two spacecraft (with the Agena docking target). Emergency return to Earth due to control system malfunctions.
Gemini-9 (USA) Thomas Stafford, Eugene Cernan

3.06.1966

3 days 21 min

 Cernan's spacewalk lasts 127 minutes. Approach to a docking target (without docking).
Gemini-10 (USA) John Young, Michael Collins

18.07.1966

2 days 22 hours 47 minutes

 Docking with the Agena-10 target. Using its engine to raise the orbit to 763 km. Approach to the Agena-8 target. Collins' spacewalk lasts 39 minutes.
Gemini-11 (USA) Charles Conrad, Richard Gordon

12.09.1966

2 days 23 hours 17 minutes

 Docking with the Agena target and using its engine to raise the orbit to 1369 km. Gordon's spacewalk lasts 163 minutes. First computer-controlled deorbit.
Gemini-12 (USA) James Lovell, Edwin Aldrin

11.11.1966

3 days 22 hours 34 minutes

 The last flight of the Gemini class ship. Docking with the Agena target. Aldrin's spacewalk lasts 5.5 hours. Deorbiting under computer control.
Soyuz-1 (USSR) Vladimir Komarov

23.04.1967

1 day 2 hours 48 minutes

 The braking parachute did not open, which resulted in the death of the astronaut.
Apollo 7 (USA) Walter  Schirra, Donn Eisel, Walter Cunningham

11.10.1968

10 days 20 hours 9 minutes

 The first manned flight of the Apollo spacecraft. The first American three-seat spacecraft. Live TV broadcast from space.
Soyuz-3 (USSR) Georgy Beregovoy

26.10.1968

3 days 22 hours 51 minutes

 Approximation with the unmanned Soyuz-2 spacecraft.
Apollo 8 (USA) Frank Borman, James Lovell, William Anders

21.12.1968

6 days 3 hours

 The first manned flight in lunar orbit. The first flight of the Saturn 5 rocket with a manned spacecraft. TV broadcast to Earth of views of the lunar surface.
Soyuz-4 (USSR) Vladimir Shatalov

14.01.1969

2 days 23 hours 21 minutes

 The first docking of two manned spacecraft (with Soyuz-5).
Soyuz-5 (USSR) Boris Volynov, Alexey Eliseev, Evgeny Khrunov

15.01.1969

3 days 54 min

 Docking with Soyuz-4. Eliseev and Khrunov went into outer space and transferred to Soyuz-4.
Apollo 9 (USA) James McDivitt, David Scott, Russell Schweickart

3.03.1969

10 days 1 hour 1 minute

 The first manned tests of the lunar module in low-Earth orbit. Schweickart's 56-minute spacewalk to test a lunar spacesuit.
Apollo 10 (USA) Thomas Stafford, John Young, Eugene Cernan

18.05.1969

8 days 3 min

 Testing the lunar module in lunar orbit. Descent to the lunar surface to a height of 15 km. Speed ​​record for manned reentry (11.0825 km/s).
Apollo 11 (USA) Neil Armstrong, Michael Collins, Edwin Aldrin

16.07.1969

8 days 3 hours 18 minutes

 First moon landing (Armstrong and Aldrin). Stay on the Moon outside the spacecraft for 152 minutes. 21.7 kg of lunar rocks were collected. Stay on the Moon for 21 hours 36 minutes.
Soyuz-6 (USSR) Georgy Shonin, Valery Kubasov

11.10.1969

4 days 22 hours 42 minutes

 Approximation with the Soyuz-7 and Soyuz-8 spacecraft. The first experiment on welding metals in space.
Soyuz-7 (USSR) Anatoly Filipchenko, Vladyslav Volkov, Viktor Horbatko

12.10.1969

4 days 22 hours 41 minutes

 Approximation with the Soyuz-6 and Soyuz-8 spacecraft. Experiments to create a space station. The first joint flight of three spacecraft and seven astronauts.
Soyuz-8 (USSR) Vladimir Shatalov, Alexey Eliseev

13.10.1969

4 days 22 hours 51 minutes

 Approximation with the Soyuz-6 and Soyuz-7 spacecraft. Experiments to create a space station.
Apollo 12 (USA) Charles Conrad, Richard Gordon, Alan Bean

14.11.1969

10 days 4 hours 36 minutes

 Second Moon landing (Conrad and Bean). Stay on the Moon outside the ship for 465 minutes (2 exits). 34.4 kg of lunar rocks were collected. Stay on the Moon for 31 hours 31 minutes.
Apollo 13 (USA) James Lovell, John Swigert, Fred Hayes

11.04.1970

5 days 22 hours 55 minutes

 The planned lunar landing was canceled due to an oxygen tank explosion. The crew returned safely to Earth, having completed a flyby of the Moon. Record distance from the Earth's surface (400,187 km).
Soyuz-9 (USSR) Andriyan Nikolaev, Vitaly Sevastyanov

1.06.1970

17 days 16 hours 59 minutes

 The flight marked the beginning of the era of long-term operation in space under zero-gravity conditions.
Apollo 14 (USA) Alan Shepard, Stuart Roosa, Edgar Mitchell

31.01.1971

9 days 2 min

 Third Moon landing (Shepard and Mitchell). Stay on the Moon outside the ship for 563 minutes (2 exits). 42.9 kg of lunar rocks were collected. Stay on the Moon for 33 hours 31 minutes.
Salyut-1 (USSR) Orbital station (uncrewed)

19.04.1971

175 days

 The first orbital station. Visit by the Soyuz-11 crew for 23 days. Entered the atmosphere and ceased to exist on 10/11/1971.
Soyuz-10 (USSR) Vladimir Shatalov, Alexey Eliseev, Nikolai Rukavishnikov

23.04.1971

1 day 23 hours 46 minutes

 Testing the docking port between the ship and the Salyut-1 station. The astronauts did not enter the orbital station.
Soyuz-11 (USSR) Georgy Dobrovolsky, Vladislav Volkov, Viktor Patsaev

16.06.1971

23 days 18 hours 22 minutes

 Docking with the Salyut-1 orbital station. Transfer of astronauts to the orbital station and stay in it for 23 days. The crew died while returning to Earth due to depressurization of the ship.
Apollo 15 (USA) David Scott, Elfrid Warden, James Irwin

26.07.1971

12 days 7 hours 12 minutes

 Fourth Moon landing (Scott and Irwin). The first use of the “lunomobile” - a wheeled vehicle. First deep space spacewalk (outside Earth orbit): Warden's spacewalk for 38 minutes. Stay on the Moon outside the ship for 18 hours 35 minutes (3 exits). 76.8 kg of lunar rocks were collected. Stay on the Moon for 66 hours 55 minutes.
Apollo 16 (USA) John Young, Thomas Mattingly, Charles Duke

16.04.1972

11 days 1 hour 51 minutes

 Fifth moon landing (Young and Duke). Stay on the Moon outside the ship for 20 hours 14 minutes (3 exits). 94.7 kg of lunar rocks were collected. Stay on the Moon for 71 hours 2 minutes.
Apollo 17 (USA) Eugene Cernan, Ronald Evans, Harrison Schmitt

7.12.1972

12 days 13 hours 51 minutes

 Sixth moon landing (Cernan and Schmitt). Stay on the Moon outside the ship for 22 hours 4 minutes (3 exits). 110.5 kg of lunar rocks were collected. Stay on the Moon for 74 hours 59 minutes. Evans' spacewalk lasts 66 minutes.
